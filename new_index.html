Making sense of data

What do we mean by 'making sense of data'? It can't be that complicated. Surely that's what graphs and stuff are for ... just shove your data into some kind of chart and the job is done, right? As it turns out - no, not really. Data visualisation is about so much more than visualising data. It's a commmunication, a set of design choices around brand and audience, and most of all it's a response. What question does your chart answer? What problem does it solve? Who will look at it and as a result feel ready to take action or make a decision? To explore this point, here's an example using some data from my real life. I spend several hours a day playing Destiny 2, an online MMORPG first-person shooter. Like many games in this genre it has a player-versus-player (PvP) mode where you can test your skills against other players in a controlled arena separate from the main story missions. Tensions run hot in this type of mode, and accusations of cheating are commonplace. Being a middle-aged gamer with below-average reflexes, I frequently come off worse in these competitions, and of course that's very frustrating. Sometimes I find myself matched against people so skilled, so godly, that I never stood a chance of beating them. When that happens I find myself hovering over the 'Report' button and asking myself, 'do I really believe they're cheating? or am I just salty about losing?'.
